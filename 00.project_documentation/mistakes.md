# 2nd Mistake

While building the silver layer, I noticed that breakdown durations arriving from the source spreadsheets were always whole minutes. Generally data generated by PLC systems don't come in full minutes, but actually in decimals.

A breakdown of 3.45 minutes therefore lands in the spreadsheet (and downstream in the warehouse) as 3. With 20 stoppages a day, that silently drops around 9 minutes of downtime per day, and the loss compounds over the week. Each individual record looks harmless, but the aggregated numbers feeding the dashboards are understated - I still need to quantify whether the drift is large enough to matter for the OEE figures.
